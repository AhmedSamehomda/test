Renewable Energy

Renewable energy refers to energy derived from naturally replenishable sources that are virtually inexhaustible and have a minimal environmental impact. It is energy obtained from resources that can be naturally replenished within a human timeframe, such as sunlight and wind

  • Solar Energy

    • Solar energy refers to the radiant light and heat from the Sun that is harvested and converted into usable forms of energy. It is a renewable and abundant source of power that can be harnessed through various technologies to generate electricity, heat water, and provide heating, cooling, and lighting for homes, businesses, and other applications.

  • Wind Energy

    • Wind energy refers to the conversion of wind power into usable electrical or mechanical energy. It is a form of renewable energy derived from the kinetic energy of moving air masses, harnessed by wind turbines or windmills to generate electricity or perform mechanical work.
